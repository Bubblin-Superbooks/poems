Insult Not The Fallen.

("Oh! n'insultez jamais une femme qui tombe.")

{XIV., Sept. 6, 1835.}

I tell you, hush! no word of sneering scorn— True, fallen; but God knows how deep her sorrow. Poor girl! too many like her only born To love one day—to sin—and die the morrow. What know you of her struggles or her grief? Or what wild storms of want and woe and pain Tore down her soul from honor?
