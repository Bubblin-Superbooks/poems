Poor grandsire, dare but to stand humbly by. You come—I go: though gloom alone my right, Blest be the destiny which gives you light.

Your fair-haired brother George and you beside Me play—in watching you is all my pride; And all I ask—by countless sorrows tried— The grave; o'er which in shadowy form may show Your cradles gilded by the morning's glow.

Pure new-born wonderer! your infant life Strange welcome found, Jeanne, in this time of strife. Like wild-bee humming through the woods your play, And baby smiles have dared a world at bay: Your tiny accents lisp their gentle charms To mighty Paris clashing mighty
