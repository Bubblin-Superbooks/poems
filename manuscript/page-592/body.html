"Ah, my poor husband! we had five before, Already so much care, so much to find, For he must work for all. I give him more. What was that noise? His step! Ah, no! the wind.

"That I should be afraid of him I love! I have done ill. If he should beat me now, I would not blame him. Did not the door move? Not yet, poor man." She sits with careful brow Wrapped in her inward grief; nor hears the roar Of winds and waves that dash against his prow, Nor the black cormorant shrieking on the shore.
