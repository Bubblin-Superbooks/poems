{November, 1870.}

If you continue thus so wan and white; If I, one day, behold You pass from out our dull air to the light, You, infant—I, so old: If I the thread of our two lives must see Thus blent to human view, I who would fain know death was near to me, And far away for you; If your small hands remain such fragile things; If, in your cradle stirred, You have the mien of waiting there for wings, Like to some new-fledged bird; Not rooted to our earth
