Of straggling clouds that trail o'erhead Like tresses from disrupted coffin-lead.

     Upon the sky
       Crape palls are often nailed
     With stars. Mine eye
       Has scared the gull that sailed

To blacker depths with shrillest scream, Still fainter, till like voices in a dream.

     My days become
       More plaintive, wan, and pale,
     While o'er the foam
       I see, borne by the gale,
