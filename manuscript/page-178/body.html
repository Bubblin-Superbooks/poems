Cease to thunder at my door; Fleeting through night's rayless region, Hither they return no more. Clanking chains and sounds of woe Fill the forests as they go; And the tall oaks cower low, Bent their flaming light before.
 On! on! the storm of wings
   Bears far the fiery fear,
 Till scarce the breeze now brings
   Dim murmurings to the ear;
 Like locusts' humming hail,
 Or thrash of tiny flail
 Plied by the fitful gale
   On some old roof-tree sere.
     Fainter now are borne
