THE SIXTH SPHINX.

Never again Cambyses earth will tread. He slept, and rotted, for his ghost had fled. So long as sovereigns live, the subjects kneel, Crouching like spaniels at their royal heel; But when their might flies, they are shunned by all, Save worms, which—human-like—still to them crawl On Troy or Memphis, on Pyrrhus the Great, Or on Psammeticus, alike falls fate.
