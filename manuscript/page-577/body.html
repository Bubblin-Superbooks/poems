The Swiss Mercenaries.

("Lorsque le regiment des hallebardiers.")

{Bk. XXXI.}

When the regiment of Halberdiers Is proudly marching by, The eagle of the mountain screams From out his stormy sky; Who speaketh to the precipice, And to the chasm sheer; Who hovers o'er the thrones of kings, And bids the caitiffs fear. King of the peak and glacier, King of the cold, white scalps— He lifts his head, at that close tread, The eagle of the Alps.

O shame! those men that march below— O ignominy
