XVI.

WHAT THEY ATTEMPT BECOMES DIFFICULT.

Bearing the sleeping Mahaud they moved now Silent and bent with heavy step and slow. Zeno faced darkness—Joss turned towards the light— So that the hall to Joss was quite in sight. Sudden he stopped—and Zeno, "What now!" called, But Joss replied not, though he seemed appalled, And made a sign to Zeno, who with speed Looked back. Then seemed they changed to stone indeed.
