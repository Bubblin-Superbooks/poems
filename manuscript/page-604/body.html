Come, sit with those who live as exiles learn: Come! Thou whom kings could conquer but not yet turn. We'll talk of "Palermo"{2}—"the Thousand" true, Will tell the tears of blood of France to you; Then by his own great Sea we'll read, together, Old Homer in the quiet summer weather, And after, thou shalt go to thy desire While that faint star of Justice grows to fire.{3}
