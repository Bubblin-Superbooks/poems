Janet shudders. "Husbandless, alone, And with two children—they have scant supplies. Good neighbor! She sleeps heavy as a stone."

She calls again, she knocks, 'tis silence still; No sound—no answer—suddenly the door, As if the senseless creature felt some thrill Of pity, turned—and open lay before.

She entered, and her lantern lighted all The house so still, but for the rude waves' din. Through the thin roof the plashing rain-drops fall, But something terrible is couched within.
